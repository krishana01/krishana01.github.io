The Problem

Half of all mental health conditions start by age 14, but most cases are undetected and untreated

Madeofmillions.com/stats

The Solution

Our overall concept stems from an apparent desperate need to give youth aged 12-19 access to mental health resources. More than a third of students surveyed responded that they don’t feel like they have an adult they can go to for help. More than a third of respondents said they would just Google for assistance in finding mental health resources, rather than go to someone personally they know. About one quarter of students said that one major roadblock for them seeking therapy is that they doubt it would help them at all. 22 actual respondents all ages 12-18.

Impact Statement

Inform teens and young adults under 19 of mental health options, as well as resources to information and therapy.

Mid Fi Wireframes

Here are some screens of our mid-fi prototypes. We did do some usability testing and some feedback from initial tests prompted us to make the “call to action” more friendly and engaging – originally it was just a fact about mental health. We also eliminated a lot of white space from around some of the icons. We made the social media links mono-chromatic so they weren’t so glaring. The “connect with therapist” piece was turned into a carousel image.

Site Mapping

One key element of the site mapping was an attempt to standardize the language used to refer to different pieces of the site. The resource is called “For Your Self” so we wanted to integrate similar pronoun usage like “My” into “My Care” and “My Settings”, which also helps to create ownership and responsibility into the care process. User flow indicates path of least resistance – how would a user go about getting help as quickly as possible? We ultimately integrated this thought process into a “step 1, step 2, step 3” piece right on the home page as a further “call to action” for users.

UI Style Tile

When creating the UI style, we wanted an overall feel of trustworthiness and positive energy. Original iterations of the color palette wound up being extremely ugly in practice (a weird avocado green shade that came across very dated and unpleasant). In some parts of the prototypes, we even wound up tinting and overlapping some of these hues to create cohesion. For UI imagery, we used image creators like StorySet and Canva, which allowed us to import our own hex values to make vector images.

Color Palette + Images

Muted colors in modern but still comforting, accessible hues.Colors were also used with varied tints, as we overlapped some transparent design elements to create cohesion between sections (Accessibility analysis showed poor feedback and contrast). Images were created using storySet and Canva.
