Final Prototype

The final protototype addressed and updated the app with the users’ feedback.

Updates

  • Used the word “add” instead of a plus sign when users add an organization to their cart.
  • Eliminated the zip code page and added it to the homepage for less steps.
  • Made the print button larger on the receipt page.
  • Made the filter component collapsible
  • Created a past donations page where users can view their reciepts and keep track of what organizations they have donated to

    Interview Plan

    We began by conducting interviews and setting up a survey in order to gather data around the pain points of getting rid of unwanted items.

    Main Research Questions

  • How do you currently get rid of unwanted items?
  • Have you had a hard time getting rid of unwanted items?
  • Are you aware of your local charities?
  • Are you environmentally concious?
  • Do you recycle your wardrobe often?

    Important Research Results

  • Donating to a charitable organization is preferred, but can be more difficult
  • Most people are environmentally concious and think it is important to be sustainable
  • Majority of people like to donate rather than throw away their items
  • Goodwill is their prefered option for donation
  • Don’t have time or are too lazy to research charitable organizations to donate to

    Competitive Analysis

    Our competivie analysis focused on Goodwill, The Green Drop, ThredUp, and Facebook Marketplace.

The Problem

Trying to get rid of unwanted items can be very time consuming and it is hard to find where to donate items sustainably. Sustainability is important to most people, but it is difficult when people are uninformed or don’t have the time to research what places accept unwanted items.

The Solution

DonateKind informs and helps users donate their unwanted items to charities, non-profits, animal shelters and churches by providing them with a quick and easy way to declutter their home sustainably and give back to their community.

Lo + Mid-fi Wireframes

After sketching out a few different versions, based on our user flow, we then combined all the best features into a mid-fidelity prototype to use for user testing.

We took all our favorite components from the low-fidelity to create a mid-fidelity prototype. We used buttons and different shades to help give a contrast to the different sections. Once completed, we created a user testing plan to see how functional the prototype was.

User Persona

For our user persona, we created Harriet. We looked at those we interviewed and those that we know care about sustainability to get inspiration. We wanted to use a city for our location because there’s a greater chance for there to be charities and organizations dedicated to helping others. We made Harriets need for this app based on something that we all deal with, which is having too much stuff. Since she’s wanting to be sustainable, we picked brands are known for being sustainable or have similar ideas.

User Flow

A user flow was created to help understand patterns users will take when using the app. This helped create a more intuitive product so that users can easily accomplish their goals.
