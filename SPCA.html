SPCA Project

Adopt. Foster. Donate. Save a Life.

    The Problem

    Adopting is a complicated and drawn out process. It can be heartbreaking to fall in love with a pet and then realize you don’t meet the requirements or the pet isn’t a good lifestyle fit.

  • Users struggle during the adoption process due to a lack of clarity of content and missing information.
  • An improved SPCA website could lead to user success.
  • Success could be measured by an increase in customers submitting adoption forms.

    User Insight

    What does the research from user research tell us?

  • Users need more details about the adoption process at the beginning of this process.
  • They need information about potential pets so they can determine if the pet will be a good fit with their lifestyle.
  • This information is necessary so they can make an informed decision.

    Research Data - Empathize

    Once interviews had been completed, our team synthesized the results into an affinity diagram. We noted the following of our findings.

  • Users were confused by the adoption process and weren’t sure of next steps.
  • 66% of users surveyed said pet adoption sites did a poor job providing the information they need.
  • Some users reported that the FAQ was cumbersome and that information was not organized well, leading to a long scrolling experience.
  • Users felt that it was difficult to find available pets and disliked that you are taken to an external site.

Future Development

From a linking perspective, the Greater Charlotte SPCA is a simple website. However, as we dug further, we found that the adoption process could become overwhelming amd convoluted without clear definition and user experience kept at the forefront.

UI Style Tile

The SPCA provides resources to users hoping to adopt a dog, as well as anybody interested in donating or fostering pets. It’s important for the site to feel welcoming and to provide information without feeling overwhelming. The colors and font choices should feel peaceful. We chose to use rounded edges for much of our iconography and formatting so that the site wouldn’t feel harsh.

Lo + Mid-Fi Wireframe

For our A/B usability testing, we interviewed several classmates as well two group family members.

Some helpful feedback that we received was that the flow of our page could be reconfigured to help draw users down the page and through the adoption funnel. We also learned that some of linking in our first module felt duplicative because it was so close to the header, leading to us changing the verbiage.

Our A/B testing gave us an opportunity to test the updated flow of our homepage (a learning from usability testing) with some classmates. Unanimously, the testing participants preferred Homepage B, so we moved forward with that architecture.

User Persona

With the insight gained through our interviews and affinity diagram, we were able to create a user persona that accurately reflects a potential user.
